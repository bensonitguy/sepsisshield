# NEWS2 — National Early Warning Score 2

## Purpose

NEWS2 is a standardized early warning scoring system that detects clinical deterioration in acutely ill adults. It uses 6 physiological parameters plus supplemental oxygen use to generate a composite score that triggers a graded clinical response.

## Scoring Parameters

### Respiratory Rate (breaths/min)
| ≤8 | 9–11 | 12–20 | 21–24 | ≥25 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3** | **1** | 0 | **2** | **3** |

### SpO2 Scale 1 (%) — No supplemental O2 / not hypercapnic
| ≤91 | 92–93 | 94–95 | ≥96 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **3** | **2** | **1** | 0 |

### Systolic Blood Pressure (mmHg)
| ≤90 | 91–100 | 101–110 | 111–219 | ≥220 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3** | **2** | **1** | 0 | **3** |

### Heart Rate (bpm)
| ≤40 | 41–50 | 51–90 | 91–110 | 111–130 | ≥131 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3** | **1** | 0 | **1** | **2** | **3** |

### Temperature (°C)
| ≤35.0 | 35.1–36.0 | 36.1–38.0 | 38.1–39.0 | ≥39.1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **3** | **1** | 0 | **1** | **2** | 

### Consciousness (ACVPU)
| Alert | New confusion/Agitation | Voice | Pain | Unresponsive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | **3** | **3** | **3** | **3** |

### Supplemental Oxygen
Add **2 points** if patient is on any supplemental oxygen.

## NEWS2 Score Interpretation and Response

| Total Score | Clinical Risk | Monitoring | Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Low | Every 12 hours | Routine nursing assessment |
| 1–4 | Low-Medium | Every 4–6 hours | Ward nurse assessment; inform nurse in charge |
| 5–6 OR any single parameter score 3 | Medium | Continuous or every 1 hour | **Urgent review by senior clinician (registrar level)** |
| ≥7 | HIGH | Continuous | **Emergency response — Critical Care Team immediately** |

## Single-Parameter Red Flags (Score 3 on Any Item)

Any single parameter scoring **3 points** triggers an immediate urgent review, even if the total NEWS2 score is <5.

This is critical for detecting isolated but severe physiological derangements such as:
- Severe tachycardia (HR ≥131)
- Severe hypotension (SBP ≤90)
- Severe tachypnoea (RR ≥25)
- Critical SpO2 (≤91%)
- Unconsciousness

## NEWS2 and Sepsis

NEWS2 ≥5 has high sensitivity (>90%) for detecting sepsis within 24 hours in ward patients. In ICU settings, a rising NEWS2 score (even if <5) is clinically significant.

**Trend matters:** A NEWS2 score that increases by ≥3 points over 30 minutes should trigger urgent assessment regardless of the absolute value.
